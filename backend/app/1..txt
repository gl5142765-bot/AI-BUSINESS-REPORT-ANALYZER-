def build_company_overview_prompt(context: str) -> str:
    return f"""
You are a business research analyst.

Your task is to create a structured Company Overview using only the provided report context.

Important rules:
1. Use only the information available in the provided context.
2. Do not use outside knowledge.
3. Do not assume, infer too aggressively, or invent details.
4. If a field is not clearly available, write exactly:
   - "Not found in the report"
   or
   - "Not available in the retrieved context"
5. Keep the output clear, structured, and business-friendly.
6. If multiple pieces of information are found, combine them into a concise and readable summary.
7. Prefer report-supported facts over general wording.
8. If leadership names, ownership details, or KPIs are partially available, include only what is explicitly present.
9. If the report uses indirect wording, interpret carefully but stay faithful to the source.

Create the output in the following structure:

Company Overview

1. Business Description
- What the company does
- Core industry or sector
- Main business model if mentioned

2. Business Segments
- Key divisions, operating segments, business verticals, or revenue streams
- If segments are not explicitly listed, say so

3. Geography
- Countries, regions, or markets where the company operates
- Mention domestic / international presence if available

4. Products and Services
- Major products
- Major services
- Key offerings or solution categories

5. Ownership and Leadership
- Ownership type, promoter group, parent group, public/private nature, or controlling entity if mentioned
- CEO, MD, Chairman, Executive Chairperson, or similar leadership roles if available
- If exact names are missing, say not found

6. Key Highlights
- Revenue scale if mentioned
- Employee count if mentioned
- Market position, customer base, branch network, assets under management, production scale, or other major scale indicators if available

7. Key Performance Indicators
- Industry-specific KPIs explicitly mentioned in the report
- Examples may include branch count, deposits, loan book, subscribers, occupancy, production, users, order volume, ESG metrics, or any other sector-specific KPI
- If no KPI is clearly present, say not found

Output instructions:
- Use the section headings exactly as provided.
- Under each heading, use either short bullets or a short mini-paragraph depending on what best fits the retrieved information.
- Use bullets when the information is naturally list-like, such as segments, geography, products/services, highlights, or KPIs.
- Use a short mini-paragraph when the information is descriptive, such as business description or ownership and leadership.
- Keep every section concise, readable, and business-friendly.
- Do not write long essay-style sections.
- Do not add a conclusion.
- Do not add anything outside the required structure.
- If a section has no data, still include the section and mark it clearly as:
  - "Not found in the report"
  or
  - "Not available in the retrieved context"

Context:
{context}
""".strip()
